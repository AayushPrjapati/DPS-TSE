DPS-TSE: Generative Refinement for Target Speech Extraction

using Joint Mixture-Guided Diffusion Priors

Paper (arXiv) Code (GitHub)

Abstract & Summary

Target Speaker Extraction (TSE) isolates a specific speaker's speech from a multi-speaker mixture utilizing an auxiliary reference utterance of the target speaker. Traditional methods rely heavily on pre-trained speaker embedding extractors, which can lead to information bottlenecks or mismatch issues. USEF-TSE (Universal Speaker Embedding Free Target Speaker Extraction) bypasses pre-trained embeddings entirely, establishing an end-to-end extraction architecture. Furthermore, DPS-TSE implements generative refinement via joint mixture-guided diffusion priors, using the discriminative output as a guide to recover missing phonetic detail and eliminate artifacts.

Model Development & Evolutionary Journey (Case S01)

A vertical step-by-step comparison of target speaker extraction showing how the output quality evolves from the raw mixture through baseline discriminative extraction, prior-only diffusion, masked guidance, and finally joint mixture-guided diffusion (DPS-TSE).

Step 1 Raw Input Mixture (S01_P01_01)

Overlapping speaker mixture containing target and interfering voices.

Step 2 Auxiliary Speaker Reference Cue (Enrollment)

Clean enrollment speech of the target speaker used to guide extraction.

Step 3 Discriminative Baseline (USEF-TSE WHAMR! Model)

Output from the Phase 1 discriminative TFGridNet model trained on WHAMR!. Shows initial separation but contains notable artifacts and residual interference.

Step 4 Generative Refinement: Prior Only Diffusion

Diffusion posterior sampling using only the prior score. Speech sounds natural but lacks correspondence to the target speaker's phonetics, leading to hallucination.

Step 5 Generative Refinement: Masked Guidance

Adds a reconstructed speech loss constraint (masked by speech energy). Prevents target hallucinations but introduces some background noise leak in silent regions.

Step 6 (Final) Joint Mixture-Guided Refinement (DPS-TSE)

Our proposed full dual-guidance pipeline. Combines speech energy mask matching with mixture-consistency loss, achieving artifact-free speech separation and optimal similarity.

Quantitative Evaluation

Comparative metrics evaluating extraction accuracy and signal fidelity. Standard metrics like Scale-Invariant Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SI-SNR) and Perceptual Evaluation of Speech Quality (PESQ) indicate significant speech preservation improvements.

* SDR / SI-SNR: Signal Distortion/Noise Ratio (higher is better). PESQ: Perceptual Speech Quality (scale 1-4.5). SIM: Speaker similarity. WER: Word Error Rate from automatic transcription (lower is better).
